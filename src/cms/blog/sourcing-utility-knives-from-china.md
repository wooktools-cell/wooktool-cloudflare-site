## Choose the right utility knife type

Common utility knife programs include heavy-duty utility knives, folding utility knives, snap-off knives, safety utility knives, and replacement blade packs. Each product type serves a different market.

Heavy-duty box cutters are often used for warehouses, cartons, and industrial work. Folding utility knives are useful for retail and DIY channels. Snap-off knives are common for stationery, general cutting, and hardware stores. Safety utility knives are suitable for warehouse and workplace programs where controlled blade exposure matters.

## Confirm blade format and material

Blade format affects both user experience and replacement demand. Buyers should confirm whether the project uses trapezoid blades, snap-off blades, 9 mm blades, 18 mm blades, or custom blade packs.

Blade material may include high-carbon steel, SK5, SK4, or SK2 depending on the product position and target price. For replacement blade programs, packaging quantity, blade color, oiling, and rust-prevention requirements should also be discussed.

## Review handle and lock design

Handle material, grip design, and lock function are important for product quality. Common handle directions include ABS, TPR, alloy, rubberized grip, folding structure, sliding track, automatic lock, retractable blade, and blade storage.

During sample review, buyers should test opening, closing, blade replacement, lock performance, cutting feel, and handle assembly.

## Packaging for retail and wholesale channels

Utility knives are often sold in blister cards, hanging cards, color boxes, display packs, or replacement blade packs. Private-label buyers should confirm logo placement, handle color, barcode labels, product warnings, carton marks, and packaging artwork.

For hardware stores, clean packaging and clear product information can improve shelf presentation. For online sellers, package size, barcode placement, and product photos may matter more.

## Quality control before shipment

Quality checks should cover blade sharpness, lock function, blade replacement fit, handle surface, assembly, packaging, barcode, carton quantity, and carton condition. Buyers can provide their own quality checklist if they have retailer or distributor standards.
